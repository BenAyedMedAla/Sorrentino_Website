AND ITS DEGRADATION





COLLAGEN: THE MATRIX OF YOUR BODY

•Collagen, a diverse family of 28 proteins, constitutes approximately 30% of the body's total protein mass, playing a vital role in providing rigidity, flexibility, traction, and integrity to various tissues such as the skin and bones. In the dermis, collagen is the predominant protein in the extracellular matrix, comprising 90% of the skin's dry mass, and is crucial for the skin's strength and structural integrity

MOLECULAR STRUCTURE & SKIN COLLAGEN DEGRADATION PROCESS

Collagen is characterized by a triple helix molecular structure primarily composed of parallel polypeptides, with every third amino acid being glycine. The most common triple chain found in collagen is PRO-HYP-GLY, with proline and 4-hydroxyproline often forming the X-Y-GLY sequence. This unique structure confers resistance to proteolytic actions, although specific proteolytic enzymes, particularly metalloproteinases (MMPs), are known for their high collagen specificity. The turnover rate of collagen in adult humans is notably low, with intracellular degradation primarily associated with physiological processes, while extracellular degradation is linked to pathological processes. Notably, MMPs, including MMP-1, MMP-2, MMP-8, MMP-12, MT1-MMP, and MT3M, are responsible for the degradation of fibrillar collagen, while MMP-3, MMP-10, and membrane-associated MMPs target type 3 collagen.





NATURAL COLLAGEN BREAKDOWN

•With aging, the natural degradation of collagen leads to a reduction in skin elasticity, the formation of wrinkles, and the loss of facial volume. This process is influenced by factors such as UV exposure, lifestyle, and genetics. Impact on Skin Structure:Collagen degradation affects the structural integrity of the skin, contributing to visible signs of aging such as sagging skin and fine lines.

BIOSTIMULATION





GENERAL DESCRIPTION

Biostimulationis a minimally invasive aesthetic treatment that uses substances or technologies to stimulate the natural processes of regeneration of the skin and subcutaneous tissue. This treatment may include the use of biostimulant substances such as vitamins, hyaluronic acid, biomimetic peptides, polynucleotides, lasers or radiofrequency to improve the appearance of the skin, reduce wrinkles, scars or other imperfections.

EFFECTS AND BENEFITS

Skin biostimulation can have several beneficial effects on the skin, including:

1. Reduction of wrinkles and fine lines:stimulation of collagen and elastin production can improve skin elasticity, thereby reducing the appearance of wrinkles and fine lines.

2. Improved skin elasticity: biostimulation can help to make the skin more toned and firm, improving its overall elasticity.

3. Scar reduction:this treatment can help reduce the appearance of acne scars, surgical scars, or other skin imperfections by promoting skin tissue regeneration.

4. Skin tone uniformity: biostimulation can help improve the appearance of skin discolorations by reducing dark spots or skin tone irregularities.

5. Enlarged pore reduction:by stimulating cell renewal and collagen production, biostimulation can help reduce the appearance of enlarged pores, thus improving the overall skin texture.

6. Improved skin radiance: thanks to the stimulation of cell regeneration, the skin may appear brighter and radiant after the biostimulation treatment.

•It is important to note that the effects and benefits of biostimulation can vary from person to person, and may require multiple sessions to achieve optimal results.

HOW?

A biostimulation session may vary slightly depending on the type of treatment and the technologies used, but usually follows a general process:



1. Initial consultation: during the first consultation, we will discuss with the patient his aesthetic goals, assess the state of the skin and explain the biostimulation treatment, including the potential risks and benefits

2. Skin preparation:before treatment, the skin will be thoroughly cleaned to remove any trace of makeup, dirt or oil and disinfection will be carried out.

3. Application of treatment:depending on the type of biostimulation chosen, treatment may involve the injection of substances such as hyaluronic acid or vitamins, the use of medical technologies, or the topical application of solutions.

4. Monitoring and follow-up:during treatment, the practitioner will carefully monitor the skin’s response and make any necessary adjustments. After the session, instructions for skin care at home may be provided and a follow-up may be planned to evaluate the results and schedule any additional sessions.
